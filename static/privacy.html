Privacy Policy

QBO Timesheet · Last updated: July 13, 2026

QBO Timesheet (the "Application") is a private, internal tool operated by Baykal Consulting solely for our own business. It is not offered to the public and has a single authorized user.

1. What the Application does

The Application connects to our own QuickBooks Online company through Intuit's APIs to create and read time-tracking entries (projects, clients, employees, service items, hours, and notes). The connection is established only after the QuickBooks account holder expressly authorizes it through Intuit's OAuth consent flow, and it operates in compliance with the Intuit Developer Terms of Service.

2. Data we access and store

  • QuickBooks data — read and written only as needed to display and record time entries. The accounting record remains in QuickBooks Online. The Application also keeps a limited activity log of timesheet changes, including the entry date, duration, person, service, customer, billable status, notes, source IP address, and prior values for edits.
  • OAuth tokens — the credentials Intuit issues when we authorize the Application are stored encrypted-in-transit and kept on our private application storage (a private server disk, or Supabase when that storage option is configured), used only to communicate with Intuit's APIs.

3. What we do not do

  • We do not sell, rent, share, or disclose QuickBooks data to third parties for marketing. Data is processed by Intuit and by the infrastructure providers used to host the private Application, such as Render and, when configured, Supabase.
  • We do not use data for advertising, profiling, or analytics.
  • We do not collect data from anyone other than our own business.

4. Security

The hosted Application is password-protected and served over HTTPS. Optional two-factor authentication is available. QuickBooks access can be revoked at any time from the QuickBooks "Apps" settings or by rotating credentials in the Intuit Developer portal.

5. Data retention and deletion

Time entries live in QuickBooks Online and follow its retention rules. The Application retains up to 2,000 timesheet-change events. Revoking the connection makes stored OAuth tokens unusable; the server operator can also delete the token, push-subscription, and audit files or corresponding Supabase rows.

6. Relationship to Intuit

The Application is independent and is not developed by, endorsed by, or affiliated with Intuit Inc. QuickBooks and QuickBooks Online are registered trademarks of Intuit Inc. Intuit's own handling of QuickBooks Online data is governed by Intuit's privacy statement.
